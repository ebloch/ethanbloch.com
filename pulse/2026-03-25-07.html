Arm Enters the Chip Business, Competing with Its Own Customers

Stratechery / Financial Times

Arm announced it will sell its own 'AGI CPU' chips—not just license IP. This is a fundamental shift from the company's 35-year-old business model as a neutral platform provider. The chips target agentic AI workloads, addressing what CEO Rene Haas calls the 'data center choking' problem: as agents generate 15x more tokens per human and run 24/7, traditional CPUs can't keep up.

The chip won't compete with Nvidia's GPUs directly but rather handles the 'orchestration' layer—the CPU-bound work of routing agent requests, managing context, and feeding those expensive accelerators. Meta is the launch partner, which signals hyperscaler demand for alternatives to Nvidia's vertically integrated stack.

Ben Thompson's analysis is sharp: Arm is calling its customers' bluff. They've threatened to leave for other architectures for years but never do. Now Arm is trading licensing revenue for the whole chip margin.

"As we move to agentic query, the number of tokens per human go up by 15x if not greater. Agents can generate requests far faster than humans, and they don't sleep. The data center is choking."

For Hiro: Direct relevance to Hiro: as you build AI agents for financial advice, you're part of the demand curve Arm is betting on. The CPU bottleneck for agentic workloads is real—this affects your infrastructure costs and the entire AI SaaS stack.

Apple Can Distill Google's Gemini Model In-House

The Information

Apple's Gemini deal goes deeper than anyone realized: Apple has full access to run Gemini in its own data centers and can distill it into smaller, on-device models. This means Apple can use Gemini as a 'teacher' model to train specialized student models that run locally on iPhones.

The implications are significant. Apple gets Gemini-quality reasoning without depending on Google's cloud at inference time. Privacy stays intact—your Siri queries can run on-device using distilled models trained on Gemini's outputs. And Apple's own Foundation Models team keeps working on original research while shipping competitive features.

Expected WWDC announcements include Siri remembering past conversations and proactive features like traffic-aware departure reminders. The slow Siri complaints may finally get answered.

"Apple can ask the main Gemini model to perform a series of tasks to produce high-quality results, including the model's step-by-step 'chain of thought' or reasoning process."

For Hiro: For Hiro: this shows the path for fintech apps on Apple's platform. If Apple can run capable models on-device, you could eventually have local financial reasoning without cloud round-trips—faster, more private, cheaper.

Federal Judge Calls Pentagon's Anthropic Ban 'Troubling' and 'Punitive'

The Information / TLDR AI

Judge Rita Lin didn't mince words in yesterday's hearing: the Pentagon's ban on Anthropic 'looks like an attempt to cripple Anthropic' rather than address legitimate security concerns. The conflict started when Anthropic requested contractual provisions ensuring Claude wouldn't be used for fully autonomous weapons or mass domestic surveillance. The Pentagon refused, then designated Anthropic in ways that have cost the company hundreds of millions in canceled contracts.

Lin noted that the government is free to stop using Claude for military purposes, but these broader punitive measures 'don't seem tailored to a national security concern.' A preliminary injunction ruling is expected within days.

This is the first major legal test of AI companies drawing ethical lines with government customers—and the government's ability to punish them for it.

"Everyone agreed that the Pentagon was free to stop using Anthropic's Claude AI for the military, but the government's actions didn't seem to be tailored to a national security concern and looked like an attempt to 'punish' Anthropic."

For Hiro: Watching this closely—it sets precedent for how AI companies can negotiate ethical constraints with their biggest potential customers. If Anthropic wins, it empowers other AI firms (including future AI fintech companies) to draw hard lines.
