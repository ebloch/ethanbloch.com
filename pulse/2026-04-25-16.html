Muni Buys Itself Two Years — But the Cliff Is Still Ahead

GrowSF

SFMTA approved a two-year, $1.5–1.6B operating budget that closes a $307M shortfall — largely by leaning on a $200M state loan and fare/parking adjustments. No immediate deep cuts. The agency has been grinding on this for a year, and it shows.

But the relief is conditional. If new transit taxes fail in November, Rachel Swan at the Chronicle reports SFMTA may eliminate up to 20 routes, cut evening service after 9pm by up to 60%, and gut cable car and F-Market service. The state loan buys time, not sustainability.

For Bay Area residents: this is the transit funding debate that will define the next few years. November's ballot will matter.

  • California Charges 21 in $267M Hospice Fraud Scheme

    GrowSF

    AG Rob Bonta charged 21 people for allegedly defrauding Medi-Cal's hospice program out of $267M — a scheme that exploited weak licensing oversight exposed in a 2022 state audit. CA has since revoked 280+ hospice licenses. The interesting angle: this is one area where Sacramento and Washington are aligned, though providers fear the broader federal-state feud could complicate future coordination.

  • SFUSD Plans Full-Year Ethnic Studies Mandate for 9th Graders

    GrowSF

    SFUSD will auto-enroll all 9th graders in a year-long ethnic studies course — despite the state's AB 101 requirement being only one semester and currently unfunded. The full-year mandate is a local policy holdover that consumes an entire elective slot for kids who are 41% proficient in 8th-grade math.
