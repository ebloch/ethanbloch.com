Lead Stories

Anthropic Built a Model Too Dangerous to Release

Ben's Bites / Anthropic

Claude Mythos represents a genuine step-change in AI capability — and Anthropic decided not to release it. The benchmarks tell the story: 77.8% on SWE-bench Pro (up from Opus's 53.4%), 82% on Terminal-Bench 2.0 (up from 65.4%). But the cybersecurity results are what made them pause.

In exploit generation tests against Firefox, Opus managed 2 working exploits out of hundreds of attempts. Mythos hit 181. It found a 27-year-old bug in OpenBSD, a 16-year-old flaw in FFmpeg, and chained Linux kernel vulnerabilities to escalate privileges — all autonomously.

Instead of a public release, Anthropic is launching Project Glasswing: $100M in compute credits plus $4M in donations to open-source security orgs, with 12 companies getting controlled access to help find vulnerabilities in critical infrastructure.

"Mythos is to Opus what Opus is to Sonnet."

Meta Superintelligence Labs Ships Muse Spark

AI News / Meta

After a year of silence and billions spent on acquisitions, Meta finally delivered. Muse Spark is their first model on a completely rebuilt stack — new architecture, new optimization, new data pipelines, done in ~9 months. The numbers are respectable: 52 on Artificial Analysis Intelligence Index (behind Gemini 3.1 Pro, GPT-5.4, and Claude Opus 4.6), 80.5% on MMMU-Pro, 39.9% on HLE.

What's interesting: Spark uses dramatically fewer reasoning tokens than competitors — 58M to run the AA index versus 120M for GPT-5.4 and 157M for Claude Opus. Meta claims >10x training efficiency gains over Llama 4 Maverick and emphasized parallel multi-agent inference for better latency. Available in meta.ai now, private API preview for partners, with promises to open-source future versions.

"Bigger models are already in development with infrastructure scaling to match... incredibly proud of the MSL team."

  • How 'Zombie Flow' Took Over Culture

    Derek Thompson

    Derek Thompson resurrects Csikszentmihalyi's concept of 'flow' — that optimal experience comes from 'body or mind stretched to its limits in voluntary effort to accomplish something difficult and worthwhile' — to diagnose what's wrong with algorithmic media. The algorithmic feed produces what researcher Shishi Wu calls 'passive flow': engagement without goals, lost self-awareness, time transformation. It mimics real flow while hollowing out its purpose. Thompson's term is sharper: zombie flow. The insight from the Shushwap tribes resonates: their elders would periodically move the entire village to new land specifically to restore difficulty and meaning to life. "It is everybody's job these days to bring the fish to the village. There is no one left whose job it is to move the village."
