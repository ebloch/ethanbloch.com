Claude Mythos: The First Model Anthropic Won't Release

Stratechery

Anthropic unveiled Claude Mythos Preview with Project Glasswing—a restricted release to ~50 companies (Apple, Microsoft, Google, Linux Foundation) for cybersecurity defense. The model found thousands of zero-days including vulnerabilities in every major OS and browser, some decades old. At $25/$125 per million tokens (5x Opus 4.6), it's expensive, but the real story is what Anthropic is signaling: frontier models may no longer mean public API access.

Ben Thompson raises the uncomfortable truth: Anthropic is essentially building strategic weapons capability. By demonstrating Mythos can attack any software while positioning themselves as defenders, they're creating a power base that could rival state actors. Thompson revisits his earlier argument that if AI reaches nuclear-weapon-level capability, Anthropic must either accept government subservience or face destruction.

"It simply isn't tolerable for the U.S. to allow for the development of an independent power structure — which is exactly what AI has the potential to undergird — that is expressly seeking to assert independence from U.S. control."

For Hiro: This is the AI alignment debate made concrete and corporate. For Hiro, it's a preview of how regulators might eventually treat powerful financial AI—especially if it can find exploits in banking systems. The 'restricted release' model could become the norm for high-capability agents.

Anthropic Hits $30B ARR—Claude Code Is Eating Software

AINews

Against OpenAI's $24B ARR and stalled ChatGPT growth, Anthropic jumped from $19B in March to $30B in April. AINews calls it a 'VERY strategic jab' at a potential IPO rival. The growth is almost entirely Claude Code—coding tools are now an order of magnitude larger than any other AI use case.

The a16z piece in today's batch quantifies this: 29% of Fortune 500 are now paying customers of AI startups. Coding, support, and search dominate. The implication is profound: AI adoption in enterprises is real, and it's concentrated in developer tools. If you're building productivity software that doesn't incorporate AI coding assistance, you're already behind.

"Anthropic was said to be '2 months and $4B ahead' of an AI 2027-style forecast, while still being valued around $380B."

For Hiro: Validation that AI agents in professional tools (coding, finance) are where the revenue is. Hiro's thesis of AI-assisted financial advice slots into the same pattern: high-value professional work where AI augmentation creates clear ROI.
