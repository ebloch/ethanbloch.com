Anthropic's Claude Mythos Is Too Dangerous to Release

Simon Willison

Anthropic isn't releasing their latest model, Claude Mythos. Instead, they're making it available only to select security partners under 'Project Glasswing' — including AWS, Apple, Microsoft, Google, and the Linux Foundation — because its cybersecurity capabilities are genuinely alarming.

The model has already found thousands of high-severity vulnerabilities in every major operating system and web browser. In one case, it wrote a browser exploit chaining four vulnerabilities with a JIT heap spray that escaped both renderer and OS sandboxes. It found a 27-year-old bug in OpenBSD's TCP stack that could crash any server with a few packets.

"On the kernel security list we've seen a huge bump of reports. We were between 2 and 3 per week maybe two years ago... and now since the beginning of the year we're around 5-10 per day depending on the days. Now most of these reports are correct."

Venture Capital Just Had Its Biggest Quarter Ever: $300 Billion

a16z

Q1 2026 was the largest quarter for venture investment ever recorded. $300 billion into roughly 6,000 startups — up 150%+ year over year. Yes, the mega-deals (OpenAI, Anthropic, xAI, Waymo combined for $188B) drive the headline. But even stripping those out, you're looking at a $112 billion quarter — still a record.

The shape of this cycle is different: beyond the foundation model labs, the billion-dollar cohort includes Cerebras (chips), Rapidus (chips), Skild AI (robots), Wayve (self-driving), Shield AI (defense). This AI cycle has a physical layer requiring factories, fabs, and fleets — part of the rotation from bits to atoms.
