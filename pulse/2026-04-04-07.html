Marc Andreessen: Why Pi + OpenClaw Is One of the 10 Most Important Software Breakthroughs Ever

Latent Space

In a sweeping interview fresh off a16z's $15B raise, Marc Andreessen makes the case that the combination of LLM + shell + filesystem + markdown + cron loop represents a fundamental architectural breakthrough. The key insight: your agent is independent of its underlying model because all state lives in files. You can swap Claude for Gemma, migrate runtimes, and the agent persists.

On why this matters: agents can now extend themselves. Tell your agent to add a capability and it will write the code, integrate the service, and evolve. Marc calls it 'an overnight success 80 years in the making' — the culmination of everything from the 1943 neural network paper through transformers, reasoning models, and now recursive self-improvement.

On the GPU shortage: even old Nvidia chips are getting more valuable because software improvements outpace depreciation. 'If I were 18, this is 100% what I would be spending all of my time on.'

"What is an agent? It turns out an agent is the following: it's a language model, it's a bash shell, it's a file system, it's markdown, and it's cron. And every part of that, other than the model, is something we already completely know and understand."

For Hiro: This is the clearest articulation yet of why agents matter architecturally. The 'agent state lives in files' pattern enables portability and self-modification — exactly the direction financial advisors need to go.

Gemma 4 Launches Under Apache 2.0 — Google's Strongest Open Model

AINews

Google dropped Gemma 4 with a true open-source Apache 2.0 license — a major shift from their previous restrictive terms. Four model sizes: E2B and E4B for devices, a 26B MoE with only 4B active parameters (the sweet spot for local inference), and the flagship 31B dense model.

The benchmarks are impressive: the 26B-A4B model runs at 162 tok/s on a single RTX 4090 at 19.5GB VRAM with 262K native context. On M4 Mac mini with 16GB? 34 tok/s. Even iPhones are running it via Swift MLX.

Ecosystem support was ready day-0: vLLM, llama.cpp, Ollama, Intel, Unsloth, and Hugging Face all shipped support immediately. François Chollet called it Google's strongest open model yet. Early gotcha: there are tokenizer bugs in llama.cpp causing gibberish output — fixes are in PR.

"Gemma 4 is the day's defining open-model release... Google's strongest open model yet."

For Hiro: A genuinely open Apache-licensed model that runs locally on consumer hardware. For anyone building agents who wants to reduce API dependency and costs, this is the hedge against Claude Code rate limits.
