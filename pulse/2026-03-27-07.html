Anthropic's Revenue Doubled in 3 Months — Is AI Still a Bubble?

Derek Thompson

Derek Thompson tackles the thorniest question in AI right now: if Anthropic just doubled its ARR from $9B to $20B between December and March — the fastest revenue growth at scale ever recorded — how can AI be a bubble?

The interview with co-founder Jack Clark goes deep on the philosophical contradictions of building technology you compare to nuclear weapons while being a private company. Clark's answer: AI is a 'factory that produces everything' — from Excel upgrades to weapons — and the job is to work with government to take dangerous capability surfaces off. It's a more nuanced framing than the usual doom-or-boom dichotomy.

On why Americans say they hate AI (net favorability of -20, below ICE): Clark admits the industry learned from social media's 'unalloyed positivity' pitch that backfired. Hence the unusual marketing of 'our product might destroy your job or the world.'

"AI is fundamentally like everything. It's like a factory that produces cars, micro scooters, animals, and nuclear weapons all at the same time."

Cursor's 'Real-Time RL' Ships Improved Models Every 5 Hours

TLDR / Cursor

Cursor revealed they're using 'real-time RL' — serving model checkpoints to production, observing user responses, and aggregating them as reward signals — to ship improved versions of Composer as often as every five hours.

This is the first clear example of a productized continuous learning loop at scale for coding agents. The implication: vertically integrated apps with high-frequency interaction data can now iterate on model quality at cadences previously unthinkable.

"Real-time RL involves using real inference tokens for training... allows them to ship an improved version of Composer as often as every five hours."
