Lead Story

The BNPL Playbook: From Hype to Banking Ambitions

Fintech Business Weekly

Jason Mikula revisits his 2020 BNPL analysis with fresh perspective. The hype cycle has played out exactly as he predicted: Klarna's $47B valuation in 2021 feels like ancient history, and the 'pay in 4' category has evolved from standalone product to customer acquisition wedge.

The real story is the banking pivot. Affirm just filed for a Nevada ILC charter and FDIC deposit insurance. Klarna has been a bank in Sweden all along. Both companies now position themselves as 'shopping destinations' first, payments second — addressing the user loyalty problem Jason flagged years ago.

The insight that matters: BNPL was never really about installment payments. It was always about acquiring young, credit-averse consumers and cross-selling them into broader financial services. The product category that seemed revolutionary is now just a feature in everyone's fintech super-app strategy.

"In retrospect, what exploded in popularity during the early days of the pandemic has come to be a wedge product/feature for a slate of (relatively) new entrants to acquire consumers and cross-sell them into a broader set of products and services."

For Hiro: This is the playbook for consumer fintech: find a wedge that resonates with underserved segments, then expand the product surface. Hiro's financial advisor positioning could learn from how BNPL players pivoted from pure payments to full financial platforms.
