The Rise of Headless Merchants: APIs That Take Payment in a Single HTTP Request

a16z crypto

In March, a marketplace opened with 60+ services designed for AI agents—not humans. SEC filing search, CAPTCHA solving, physical letter mailing, image generation—all priced per API call, payable in cards, stablecoins, or Lightning. In the first week: 894 agents, 31,000 transactions.

The Machine Payments Protocol (MPP) from Stripe and Tempo embeds payment directly into HTTP requests. No checkout page, no accounts, no sales team. Just endpoints and prices. The payment is the authentication.

This inverts business building entirely. The agent buyer will never see your website. It sees your API docs, pricing, and uptime. A headless merchant with clean docs beats one with a beautiful site and mediocre API.

"Commerce used to happen in places: a store, a website, an app. Headless merchants move commerce to moments. The instant an agent needs a capability it doesn't have, it transacts."

For Hiro: This is exactly the agentic commerce infrastructure Hiro will need. When financial agents need services—market data, tax calculations, compliance checks—they'll pay per request. The business model implications are huge: per-request pricing may replace subscriptions for API services.

Public Lets You Deploy AI Agents to Execute Your Trades

Bloomberg Money Stuff

Public, a retail brokerage, is rolling out AI agents that automate trading tactics. Type in a strategy—'Whenever SPY drops 3% or more, invest $10,000 at the next market open'—and the agent executes it. Buy protective puts when oil spikes, sweep cash into higher-yield assets, add stop-losses automatically.

Matt Levine's insight is sharp: retail investors have always behaved predictably (dip-buying, panic-selling), but now that behavior is being automated into rules. Once enough retail investors use AI agents with similar strategies, hedge funds can predict and front-run the automated flows.

"If retail investing becomes increasingly agentic, the agents might tend to automate stereotypical retail behaviors. These days, the most stereotypical retail behavior probably is buying the dip, and maybe that is the first one that will be automated."

For Hiro: Direct application to Hiro's thesis: when financial advice becomes agentic, the market microstructure changes. Automated retail flows become predictable. Also shows where retail fintech is heading—from robo-advisors to AI agents that execute.
