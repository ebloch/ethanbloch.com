Claude Mythos Is the Cyber Moment Everyone Warned About

Zvi / TLDR AI

Anthropic's newest model found 27-year-old vulnerabilities in foundational open-source code that millions of tests and countless human experts missed. The company won't release it publicly until "our most important software is in a much stronger state" — instead, Project Glasswing gives limited access to ~40 companies to patch systems before this capability proliferates.

The ChinaTalk podcast with Ben Buchanan (former White House AI advisor) and Michael Sulmeyer (former Assistant Secretary of Defense for Cyber) frames it starkly: this is the automated vulnerability discovery that security researchers imagined for a decade, finally arriving. The offensive implications are clear — a state with Mythos and adversaries without it could enjoy "open season." But Anthropic is betting that a head start for defenders can tilt the balance.

Ben Thompson at Stratechery adds another angle: Mythos being gated isn't just about safety — it's about opportunity cost and pricing power. Anthropic is already compute-constrained serving current models. Limiting access to high-paying enterprise customers while stopping distillation protects both margins and lead time.

"We thought a lot of software was secure, but then again, we thought some of the software that Mythos found vulnerabilities in was secure as well. And clearly it wasn't."

For Hiro: This is the clearest signal yet that AI capabilities are hitting inflection points with real-world security implications. If foundation model capabilities are diverging this sharply, the "build vs. buy" calculus for AI features gets more complex. Also a reminder that security posture matters — the financial sector is already getting briefings from the Fed and Treasury.

Perplexity Launches AI-Powered Personal CFO

TLDR Fintech

Perplexity introduced a personal finance hub connecting bank accounts, credit cards, loans, and investments via Plaid. Users can analyze their full financial picture through natural language queries — generating net worth trackers, budget models, and cash flow forecasts using real-time data.

This isn't a dashboard. It's aggregation + analysis + advisory in one AI-native interface. The positioning directly challenges traditional finance apps by collapsing multiple product categories into conversational interactions.

"The move positions Perplexity as an AI-native alternative to traditional finance apps, blending aggregation, analysis, and advisory into a single interface."

For Hiro: This is exactly Hiro's thesis, executed by a well-funded competitor. Perplexity has distribution (their search product) and now financial data access. The question: is the personal finance UX right, or is there room for something more specialized? My take: Perplexity is going wide, Hiro should go deep.
