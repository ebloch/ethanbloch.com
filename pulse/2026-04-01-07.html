Claude Code Source Leak Reveals the Harness Is the Moat

Stratechery

Anthropic accidentally shipped a 60MB source map file in their npm package, exposing 500k+ lines of Claude Code's TypeScript codebase to the world. Within hours, the code was mirrored, analyzed, and forked thousands of times — and the discoveries are illuminating.

The leak revealed a sophisticated agent harness with features like Kairos (always-on autonomous mode), a three-layer memory architecture with 'dream mode' for memory consolidation, subagents that leverage prompt caching for essentially free parallelism, and even a Tamagotchi-style '/buddy' pet system that Anthropic apparently hoped would generate 'sustained Twitter buzz.'

Ben Thompson's key insight: the entities that will benefit most aren't OpenAI or Google, but companies like Cursor and Microsoft who are building harnesses for Claude. The real secret sauce isn't in this code — it's in the integration of harness and model. My take: this confirms what many suspected — the 'wrapper' layer that VCs dismissed is actually hard engineering, not trivial glue code.

"The most important differentiator is not just the harness but rather the integration of harness and model."

For Hiro: Directly relevant to Hiro's architecture. The leak validates the 'harness matters' thesis — financial agent quality will come from orchestration, memory, and tool integration, not just model capability.

OpenAI's $122B Round: The Largest Fundraise in Human History Closes

On my Om

OpenAI closed its mega-round at $122 billion committed capital (up from $110B announced in February) at an $852 billion post-money valuation. Amazon ($50B tied to an 8-year AWS contract), Nvidia (compute, not cash), and SoftBank anchor the round, with the FOMO gang — a16z, Sequoia, BlackRock, Fidelity — piling in at the last minute to ride the IPO pop.

The circular financing hasn't gone away: Bloomberg is now officially calling it 'circular financing.' But the real story is the soft IPO mechanics. OpenAI announced $3B from individual investors — customers of the same banks (JPMorgan, Goldman, Citi) extending their credit facility and positioning for IPO underwriting. And they're getting into ARK ETFs pre-IPO, letting Cathie Wood's retail base feel like they have skin in the game before OpenAI even files.

Buried in the noise: OpenAI is shutting down Sora and pivoting to a 'superapp' for developers with coding assistants at the center. Enterprise is where Anthropic is eating their lunch.

"You don't want to be the one without a chair when the music stops."

For Hiro: The enterprise pivot is notable — if OpenAI is chasing Claude Code's success, that validates the coding agent thesis. Also a lesson in creative financing for Hiro's future rounds.
