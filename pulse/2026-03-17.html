Global Stocks Post Worst Month in 3.5 Years as Middle East Conflict Escalates

Iran set a massive UAE gas field ablaze, pushing Brent crude above $103 and US diesel past $5/gallon for the first time since 2022. While the S&P 500 is down "only" 2.6% (benefiting from US energy exporter status), the real carnage is global: Japan -7.9%, South Korea -9.7%, Indonesia -14%. Bloomberg's Mark Cudmore warns that US market "resilience" is masking broader investor complacency—and facilitating much greater declines ahead.
