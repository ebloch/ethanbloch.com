Anthropic Hits $30B Run-Rate But Faces a Compute Crunch

Stratechery

Anthropic's revenue has surged past $30 billion in annualized revenue—up from $9B at end of 2025—possibly surpassing OpenAI. The company doubled its $1M+ enterprise customers from 500 to 1,000 in under two months. But this explosive growth comes with a serious problem: they can't get enough compute.

Google has the most AI compute, mostly TPUs. Anthropic just signed a deal with Google and Broadcom for 3.5 gigawatts of TPU capacity starting 2027. Ben Thompson does back-of-envelope math suggesting this covers maybe $100B in incremental revenue—which might not be enough given their growth curve.

The strategic alignment is fascinating: Google wins even if Anthropic beats them in enterprise. Every Claude customer running on Google Cloud means Google's capex pays off, just at slightly lower margins. And a strong Anthropic weakens OpenAI, Google's real consumer AI rival.

"A world where Anthropic wins enterprise over OpenAI both weakens Google's primary competitor in the consumer space, and gives Google the money and future certainty to continue to spend OpenAI into the ground."

Anthropic Cuts Off Claude Subscriptions for Third-Party Tools Like OpenClaw

ben's bites

Anthropic announced Claude Code subscriptions can no longer be used for third-party tools like OpenClaw—now requiring separate pay-as-you-go API billing. The stated reason: capacity. Tools like OpenClaw put 'an outsized strain' on Anthropic's systems, which 'weren't built for the usage patterns of these third-party tools.'

The developer community is confused and frustrated. Hamza Mostafa, a former OpenAI agents team member, is now considering switching to OpenAI's models for his OpenClaw agent. Meanwhile, OpenClaw's creator Peter Steinberger (recently acqui-hired by OpenAI) is working to make GPT-5.4 work 'like Opus' in the tool.

"Using Claude subscriptions with third-party tools isn't permitted under our Terms of Service, and they put an outsized strain on our systems. Capacity is something we manage thoughtfully, and we need to prioritize customers using our core products."
