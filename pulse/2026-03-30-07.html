Claude Mythos Leaks: Anthropic's Next Frontier Model Is Too Expensive to Ship

TLDR AI / The Information

Anthropic accidentally published details of 'Mythos,' a new model tier that sits above Opus in capability. The models score dramatically higher on coding, academic reasoning, and cybersecurity benchmarks. But here's the catch: Mythos is so compute-intensive that Anthropic says it needs to make it 'much more efficient before any general release.'

This comes as Anthropic's success is creating infrastructure headaches. Revenue more than doubled to $19B annualized in the first two months of 2026 (almost entirely from Claude Code), but reliability is suffering as server capacity can't keep pace. The company limited some Claude Code customers during peak hours last week, and OpenAI immediately responded by doubling Codex usage limits.

Anthropic is now discussing an IPO that could happen as soon as Q4, potentially raising up to $60B. Google is reportedly nearing a deal to finance a multibillion-dollar data center for Anthropic, though capacity won't come online until late 2026 at earliest.

"Claude Mythos is 'a large, compute-intensive model' that is 'very expensive to serve, and will be very expensive for customers to use.'"

Every Building You've Ever Been In Was Designed By Software Built in 1997

a16z

a16z makes the case that the $13 trillion AEC (architecture, engineering, construction) industry is ripe for AI disruption. Autodesk's Revit has 95%+ market share in building design software, does ~$3B ARR, and hasn't meaningfully innovated in decades. Engineers spend 35% of their time—14+ hours per week—on non-productive activities like hunting for project info and fixing coordination failures.

The piece outlines three attack vectors: replace Revit directly (hardest, but Motif is trying), build around Revit's gaps (LightTable doing AI document review), or go after the services budget that sits on top of Revit (Endra automating MEP design). The key insight: MEP design is a $150B global services market where most work is rule-based and rote. AI can unlock latent demand from firms sitting on backlogs they can't serve.

"The entire built world is still running on software built in 1997. That's about to change."

The Surprising Math of Intergenerational Wealth

Verdad

Gregory Clark (UC Davis economist, author of 'The Son Also Rises') presents research that upends the 'shirtsleeves to shirtsleeves in three generations' wisdom. Using 447,000 English lineages from 1600-2026, he finds descendants of the wealthy remain wealthy after five generations and have above-average wealth for 10-12 generations (300+ years).

But here's the twist: this persistence is not caused by inherited wealth. When he isolates the effect of actual inheritance (using family size as a natural experiment), inherited fortunes do disappear in ~3 generations, just as conventional wisdom suggests. The persistence comes from something else: human capital—education, connections, skills, values—that wealthy families transmit across generations.

"The descendants of the elite who managed to survive the Communist Revolution's violent land expropriations have re-emerged as key elements of the new wealthy class of China."
