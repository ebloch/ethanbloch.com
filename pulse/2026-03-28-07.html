Banks Are Playing Both Sides of the Private Credit Meltdown

FinTech Collective

Private credit spent years positioning itself as the alternative to bank intermediation. Now the banks are back — JPMorgan has committed $50B to direct private loans while simultaneously tightening financing for software-heavy funds and helping hedge funds bet against private credit exposure.

The liquidity squeeze is real: Apollo, Ares, BlackRock, and Morgan Stanley are all limiting redemptions. Bloomberg estimates investors sought $13B in withdrawals this quarter but only got two-thirds back, with $4.6B stuck behind fund gates. The concentration risk is stark — JPMorgan estimates software accounts for 30% of private credit loans vs. 10% for bank-originated debt.

"Since the 2008-09 financial crisis, private credit has been taking market share from the big banks, and Dimon has been among those warning about potential dangers lurking in the unregulated industry. Now, JPMorgan and other banks are paring back risk and sensing opportunity."

H100 Prices Are Melting UP, Not Down

AINews

A 4-year-old chip worth more than when it launched? That's the H100 market right now. After briefly bottoming out post-DeepSeek R1, rental prices have surged since December 2025. The driver: reasoning models and agents consuming inference at unprecedented rates.

The economic implications are significant: data center business models that assumed 4-7 year depreciation cycles are now facing upward price pressure. Better reasoning models + better inference software = the same chip is genuinely more valuable than before.

"The utility of a 4 year old chip now with much better reasoning models and inference software means the chip itself is much more valuable than initial 4-7 year depreciation schedules had assumed."
