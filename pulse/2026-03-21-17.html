Open Agentic Commerce: The HTTP Moment for AI Agents

a16z crypto

The a16z crypto team makes a sweeping argument: we're witnessing an economic phase change comparable to the open web vs. AOL battle of the '90s. ChatGPT checkout is the AOL version — curated catalogs, BD deals, walled gardens with better UX. But the real shift is Open Agentic Commerce: protocols like x402 (Coinbase) and mpp (Tempo/Stripe) that let agents pay for anything permissionlessly.

The key insight is that ads worked because humans get distracted. LLMs don't. Stack Overflow traffic is down 75% since GPT-4, tech news down 60%. The ad-supported web is dying, and what replaces it is micropayments via stablecoins — finally solving the fixed-fee problem that killed micropayments in 1997.

The piece introduces AgentCash as a discovery layer: a single balance with access to every API on the internet. Merchants register on x402scan.com or mppscan.com and instantly reach 2,000+ agents. No BD, no whitelist, just permissionless standards.

"An agent that can only buy from pre-approved merchants is an employee with a corporate card restricted to three vendors. An agent with open protocols is an entrepreneur with a bank account."

For Hiro: This is the infrastructure layer Hiro needs to watch closely. If agents can compose capabilities and pay for services autonomously, the fintech stack changes fundamentally. The supply chain example — an agent noticing a 15% price increase, finding alternatives, negotiating, and switching suppliers before the owner's morning workout — that's the future of financial automation.

AI Is Collapsing the Discovery Economy

Om Malik

Om Malik uses the Michael Smith streaming fraud case ($8M stolen via AI bots) as a warning shot for something much bigger. The entire digital economy — Spotify's Discover Weekly, YouTube suggestions, Amazon's product feed, Facebook's news feed — runs on signals of human behavior. Those signals are now trivially fakeable.

The numbers are stark: Apple Music flagged 2 billion fraudulent streams in 2025. Deezer receives 60,000 fully AI-generated tracks daily, with 85% of AI music streams classified as fraudulent. And here's the kicker: bot traffic surpassed human traffic on the internet in 2024 for the first time in a decade (51% bots). Cloudflare blocked 13 trillion bad bot requests in 2025.

Om's question is the right one: "Whether it is Spotify directing culture, Amazon directing commerce, or Facebook directing conversations... I have yet to hear from any of their leadership on how they plan to redesign what they do because of the coming onslaught of fake signals."

"At what point does fraudulently-obtained popularity become real popularity? There's no clean line."

For Hiro: This connects directly to the trust problem in fintech. If recommendation systems are compromised by fake signals, so are financial product comparisons, investment research, and advisor ratings. Hiro's edge could be verified, provable signals in a world drowning in synthetic ones.
