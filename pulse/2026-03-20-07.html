Every AI Lab Serious About Developers Has Bought Their Own Devtools

AINews

OpenAI's acquisition of Astral completes a fascinating pattern: Google DeepMind bought what became the Antigravity team last July, Anthropic acquired Bun in December, and now OpenAI grabs the popular Python tooling company. Each major lab is building its own developer toolchain in-house.

This comes as Fidji Simo explicitly drops OpenAI's "side quests" like Shopping (Walmart reporting awful 1/3 conversion rates) to prioritize Enterprise and Coding. The ChatGPT and Codex apps are being unified into a single "superapp."

Meanwhile, Cursor shipped Composer 2 — their first model built on continued pretraining feeding a stronger base into RL. The numbers are compelling: $0.50/M input and $2.50/M output, with 61.3 on CursorBench and 73.7 on SWE-bench Multilingual.

"Astral joins OpenClaw and (to a lesser extent) gpt-oss and Whisper in OpenAI's growing list of top tier open source AI projects."

For Hiro: The developer tools land grab has major implications for Hiro. If AI labs are vertically integrating devtools, the same will likely happen in financial tools. The "own your infrastructure" lesson applies to Hiro's stack.

Iran War Reshapes Rate Expectations: No Fed Cuts This Year

Bloomberg

Three weeks into the Iran conflict, the bond market is being turned upside down. Central bankers have dashed expectations for easier monetary policy — traders are now pricing in zero chance the Fed will cut rates this year.

Short-term bonds plunged, with the UK becoming an epicenter of turmoil as 10-year yields hit their highest since 2008. The Bank of England signaled it "stands ready to act" against inflation, sparking bets on rate hikes as soon as next month.

Gold is getting hammered too — down 7% on the week and 11% in March. If that holds, it's the biggest monthly drop since 2008. Brent crude remains above $100.

"There was a consensus view that this was going to be over relatively quickly. The fear is now finally in the market that this could last a lot longer."

For Hiro: This macro shift matters for Hiro's advice engine. The entire rate landscape clients were planning around has inverted. Financial planning assumptions from a month ago are obsolete.
