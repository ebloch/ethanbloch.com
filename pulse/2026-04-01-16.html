OpenAI Raises $122B Privately — More Than the Largest IPO Ever

Bloomberg (Money Stuff)

OpenAI completed a $122 billion funding round at an $852 billion valuation, dwarfing SpaceX's confidential IPO filing that would raise $75 billion (itself the largest IPO ever). The kicker: OpenAI raised $3 billion from individual investors through bank wealth channels and ARK ETFs.

Matt Levine's observation is sharp: private markets are now definitively the new public markets. You can raise more, faster, with less scrutiny. But there's a catch — OpenAI shares are now struggling on secondary markets as sophisticated investors pivot to Anthropic at its 'cheaper' $380 billion valuation.

"We literally couldn't find anyone in our pool of hundreds of institutional investors to take these shares... Meanwhile, buyers have indicated they have $2 billion of cash ready to deploy into Anthropic."

For Hiro: Directly relevant to fundraising strategy and market positioning. The AI infrastructure funding arms race is real, but so is the risk of raising at valuations that leave nowhere to go.

Prediction Markets Are Eating Crypto's Lunch

The Information

Kalshi just doubled to $22B valuation while Bitcoin sits at half its October peak. Monthly trading volume on prediction markets exploded from $151M to $20B year-over-year. The thesis: prediction markets have replaced crypto as the adrenaline-driven trading destination.

The irony is delicious — crypto enabled prediction markets through stablecoins (the 'least sexy' part of crypto that actually has utility), and now prediction markets are cannibalizing crypto's retail trading base. Even crypto exchanges are pivoting: Coinbase, Crypto.com, and Gemini have all launched their own prediction markets.

"On Kalshi, bettors assigned just a 32% chance that bitcoin will cross $100,000 before October 2027. The crypto currency was at $126,000 last October and now sits at half that level."

For Hiro: Worth watching — prediction markets represent a new financial product category that's capturing retail engagement. The regulatory battles will shape what's possible.
