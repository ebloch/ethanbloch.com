Leaky Abstractions Are the Next Startup Map

a16z

The strongest essay in today's batch argues that the modern world is not collapsing; its abstractions are leaking. Online tax collection, energy systems, money, and the internet used to hide messy implementation details behind clean interfaces. Now governments, consumers, and institutions can see the machinery, and they increasingly try to grab the controls directly.

That matters for Ethan because financial advice is full of these leaky interfaces: banks, payroll, taxes, brokerages, credit rails, identity, custodians, market data. Hiro's product opportunity is not just better recommendations; it is helping users navigate the exposed plumbing when the old clean abstractions stop feeling reliable. My take: the best AI finance products will look less like dashboards and more like calm interpreters of brittle systems.

"the world’s abstractions will leak; a lot. So get to know some plumbers."

Why it matters: Directly maps to Hiro: durable AI advice depends on integrating across messy financial infrastructure, not pretending the interface is clean. My take: this is a product strategy essay disguised as a civilization essay.
