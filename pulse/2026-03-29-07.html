Lead Story

Did Stripe Subsidiary Bridge Ship Trucks to Venezuela?

Fintech Business Weekly

Jason Mikula at Fintech Business Weekly found shipping records listing Bridge Ventures Inc. as the seller/shipper of 12 Mitsubishi trucks to Venezuela in late 2023/early 2024. The recipient? Thundernet, a Venezuelan ISP owned by Grupo Nemer — a family with documented ties to Hugo Chávez's brothers and alleged involvement in a Venezuelan payment processing corruption scheme.

Bridge denies any connection, calling it "some clerical error or confusion around a common name." But this comes on the heels of the Kontigo scandal (Bridge's relationship with a Venezuelan crypto exchange) and while Bridge is trying to operationalize an OCC-chartered trust bank.

The compliance implications are serious: Venezuela faces broad U.S. sanctions, and the Nemer family's sprawling 50+ company structure across Venezuela, Panama, and the US makes beneficial ownership verification nearly impossible. Even if Bridge is telling the truth about non-involvement, the pattern of Venezuela-adjacent controversies is becoming a problem.

"Whatever this is about, it has nothing to do with us: Bridge had no involvement in this shipment or any associated payment activity. The only explanation we can think of is some clerical error or confusion around a common name like Bridge."

Why this matters: Critical reading for anyone watching the stablecoin infrastructure space. Bridge was the poster child for "stablecoins enable emerging market access" — but if they can't get compliance right, it validates every skeptic's concern about the space. Also a lesson in how investigative journalism can crater fintech valuations overnight.
