Simon Willison: 'My Ability to Estimate Software Is Broken'

Simon Willison's Newsletter

In a wide-ranging conversation on Lenny's Podcast, Simon Willison crystallizes what many of us have felt but couldn't articulate: 25 years of software estimation expertise is now useless. A task he'd estimate at two weeks might now take 20 minutes—the 'crufty coding things' AI handles effortlessly.

But the real insight is where the bottlenecks have moved. It's not writing code anymore—it's testing, validating, and figuring out which of three rapid prototypes is actually right. Simon's now prototyping three different approaches to every feature because 'that takes very little time.' The meta-skill of product thinking is suddenly the scarce resource.

The piece also introduces the 'dark factory' concept from StrongDM: nobody writes code, and increasingly, nobody reads it either. Six months ago Simon thought this was crazy. Now 95% of his code is AI-generated.

"It used to be, you'd come up with a spec and you hand it to your engineering team. And three weeks later, if you're lucky, they'd come back with an implementation. And now that maybe takes three hours."

For Hiro: This is the lived reality Hiro needs to design for. If coding agents make implementation trivial, the value shifts to taste, judgment, and—critically—knowing what to build. Financial advice is the same: the hard part isn't executing trades, it's knowing which trades matter.

Gemma 4 Arrives: Apache 2.0, Multimodal, and Shockingly Capable for Size

Google DeepMind / TLDR AI

Google DeepMind dropped four new open models under Apache 2.0—a huge licensing upgrade from Gemma 3. The lineup: 2B, 4B, 31B dense, and a 26B MoE with only 4B active parameters. All support vision; the smaller ones add native audio input.

The 31B dense model ties with Kimi K2.5 and GLM-5 on Arena rankings but with far fewer total parameters. The architecture is dense with tricks: hybrid local/global attention, per-layer embeddings, KV-cache sharing, and proportional RoPE. Day-zero support landed in llama.cpp, Ollama, vLLM, and LM Studio.

Real-world performance looks strong. Simon Willison got 'probably the best pelican I've seen' from the 26B-A4B running locally. The 4B model reportedly performs between GPT-3.5 and GPT-4o—on a laptop.

"The 31B dense variant ties with Kimi K2.5 and GLM-5 for the world's top open models, but with far fewer total parameters."

For Hiro: Apache 2.0 is huge for Hiro. Open models with this quality level mean local inference for privacy-sensitive financial data becomes viable. The on-device models (E2B, E4B) could run financial assistants without cloud dependencies.
