Claude Auto Mode Released in Research Preview

TLDR AI

Anthropic just released autonomous agent mode for Claude, and it's significant. This isn't just another chatbot feature — it's Claude executing multi-step workflows independently, with built-in safeguards that pause for human approval on sensitive actions.

The architecture matters here: rather than 'AI does everything unsupervised,' Anthropic is betting on a middle path where the model proposes actions, executes routine steps, but escalates appropriately. It's the exact pattern you'd want for financial operations — automate the boring stuff, surface the decisions that need human judgment.

My take: This is the template for how AI agents should work in regulated industries. The 'autonomous but supervised' pattern is exactly what Hiro needs for client operations. Watch how Anthropic handles the edge cases.

For Hiro: Direct implications for Hiro's architecture. Autonomous agents with guardrails is the pattern for AI in finance — not full automation, not just chat. This is the middle path.

Mercury Launches AI-Powered Finance with MCP Integration

Mercury

Mercury just shipped AI insights with MCP (Model Context Protocol) integration, bringing automated finance operations and smarter transaction categorization to their banking product. They're positioning AI as the interface layer for financial data.

This is competitive intelligence for Hiro. Mercury has a huge SMB customer base and they're essentially saying 'your AI assistant can now talk directly to your bank.' The MCP angle is clever — they're making Mercury data accessible to any AI tool that speaks the protocol, not just their own features.

My take: Mercury is signaling where fintech is headed. The bank account becomes an MCP server; your AI assistant becomes the primary interface. Hiro should be paying attention to how customers respond to this.

For Hiro: Competitive landscape signal. Mercury is moving fast on AI + banking integration. Watch adoption patterns — this shows what features customers are ready for.
