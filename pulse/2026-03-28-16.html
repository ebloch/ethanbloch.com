H100 Prices Are Melting... UP

AINews

In a twist nobody expected, H100 rental prices have reversed course and are climbing sharply since December 2025. The standard GPU depreciation cycle has been completely upended — a 4-year-old chip is now worth MORE than when it launched.

The drivers: chip shortage, the reasoning model/agent inflection point from December 2025, and dramatically improved inference software that makes older hardware far more capable than initial depreciation schedules assumed. Dylan from Dwarkesh confirms H100s are worth more today than 3 years ago.

"The utility of a 4 year old chip now with much better reasoning models and inference software means the chip itself is much more valuable than initial 4-7 year depreciation schedules had assumed."

For Hiro: Direct implications for infrastructure decisions. If GPU economics are inverting, the calculus on build vs. buy for inference changes dramatically.

Private Credit's Liquidity Squeeze: $5B Trapped Behind Fund Gates

FinTech Collective

The private credit illiquidity chickens are coming home to roost. Apollo, Ares, BlackRock, and Morgan Stanley have all limited redemptions — investors sought ~$13B in withdrawals this quarter but recovered only two-thirds. The scary stat: JPMorgan estimates software accounts for 30% of private credit loans outstanding vs. ~10% for bank-originated debt.

The banks are playing both sides beautifully — financing private credit, pulling back risk when conditions tighten, AND helping clients position against it. JPMorgan has $50B committed to direct private loans while simultaneously building strategies for hedge funds to bet against the sector.

"Private credit spent years casting itself as an alternative to bank intermediation. But when conditions tighten, the banks still shape the outcome."

For Hiro: Software concentration in private credit is a canary in the coal mine for fintech valuations broadly. If software borrowers can't service debt through AI disruption, ripple effects hit the whole sector.
