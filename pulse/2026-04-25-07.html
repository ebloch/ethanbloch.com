DeepSeek V4: Open Models Finally Have Real 1M Context

AINews / Latent Space

After months of anticipation, DeepSeek dropped V4 in two tiers: Pro (1.6T total params, 49B active) and Flash (284B total, 13B active). The headline achievement isn't the raw parameter count — it's the 1M token context window, achieved through new Compressed Sparse Attention (CSA) and Heavily Compressed Attention (HCA) techniques that shrink KV cache to just 9.62 GiB per sequence, down from 83.9 GiB in V3.2. At 1M tokens, the model requires only 27% of FLOPs and 10% of KV cache memory vs its predecessor.

Benchmarks place V4 Pro as #2 open-weights reasoning model (behind Kimi K2.6) and #1 in agentic real-world work across open models, leading on the GDPval-AA benchmark at 1554. It's roughly Opus 4.5-tier, below closed frontier (GPT-5.4, Opus 4.7, Gemini 3.1 Pro) but within striking distance. Pricing is aggressive: $1.74/$3.48 per 1M input/output for Pro, and $0.14/$0.28 for Flash — with DeepSeek hinting prices could fall further once Huawei Ascend 950 supernodes scale in H2. Both Base and Instruct weights released under MIT license with day-0 support from vLLM, Together, Baseten, and others.

"They've completed their quest: Solid Ultra-Long Context."

Flash at $0.14/$0.28 per 1M tokens with 1M context is a genuinely different cost curve for Hiro's inference layer. If you're running financial analysis agents over long documents — SEC filings, earnings transcripts, portfolio histories — this changes the economics. Worth benchmarking against Gemini Flash for long-context financial tasks.

Someone (Allegedly) Hacked a Thermometer to Win $21K on Polymarket

FinTech Collective

On April 15, the temperature sensor at Paris Charles de Gaulle airport spiked five degrees in twelve minutes — no nearby station registered anything similar. Just before the anomaly, a Polymarket account called 'xX25Xx' placed ~$120 in bets against an 18°C daily peak. The spike paid ~$21,000. The account has since deleted its username. Nine days earlier, a different account made nearly $14,000 on a 21°C temperature bet. Météo-France filed a tampering complaint with airport police; Polymarket moved its Paris reference station to Le Bourget.

The stakes were real beyond the bets: the CDG sensor calibrates altitude, fuel use, and runway selection for France's largest airport. The two Paris temperature contracts drew ~$1.4 million in combined wagers — more than double typical April volume. This is also the same week a US Army soldier was arrested for using classified intelligence to place lucrative bets on Polymarket, signaling what FinTech Collective called "a new era of stricter government enforcement of the wildly popular betting platforms."

"At Charles de Gaulle, someone apparently decided that didn't apply to thermometers."

A masterclass in why prediction market contract design is hard. When the settlement oracle is a physical sensor accessible in the real world, you've created an incentive to tamper with it. As Kalshi, Polymarket, and others scale, the attack surface for manipulation scales with them. Worth watching for any fintech building on prediction market data as signals.

  • Anthropic Confesses: Three Bugs Made Claude Code Feel Dumber for a Month

    AINews / Latent Space

    Anthropic published a rare postmortem admitting three separate bugs degraded Claude Code's performance from early March through April 20th: a silent downgrade of reasoning effort from high to medium (March 4, reverted April 7); a caching bug that caused Claude to forget its reasoning history (March 26); and a system prompt change that limited responses to 25 words between tool calls (April 16, the most damaging for coding quality, reverted April 20). All three affected different traffic slices. As an apology, Anthropic reset usage limits for all subscribers and shipped the fix in v2.1.116+. If you were frustrated with Claude Code over the last 6 weeks — this is why. Worth re-evaluating on Hiro tasks now that it's fixed.
