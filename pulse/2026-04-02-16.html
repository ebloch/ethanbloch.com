Anthropic's 'Mythos' Model Could Give Hackers an Edge Over Defenders

The Information

Anthropic is briefing cybersecurity researchers on an upcoming AI model that could fundamentally shift the offense-defense balance in security. The model is said to be significantly better at code generation and review — which also means it's better at finding exploits.

The scariest detail: In a demo, Claude Code found a critical vulnerability in Ghost (an open source newsletter platform with 13 years and zero critical CVEs) in just hours. The model didn't just find it — it could help hackers move through networks undetected, map infrastructure, and encrypt data before defenders respond.

Defenders are now using 'ungated' versions of these same models — the raw models without safety guardrails — to hack their own customers preemptively. It's an AI arms race, and the asymmetry favors attackers: they only need to succeed once.

"We now believe the new models are essentially the best cybersecurity researchers in the world, and that's a problem."

For Hiro: This is the shape of AI risk that's actually happening — not sci-fi scenarios but concrete capability jumps in adversarial domains. For Hiro, it reinforces why security-first design matters as AI agents get access to financial systems.

Wall Street Creates Prediction Market Structured Notes

Bloomberg (Matt Levine)

Marex just created the first bond tied to prediction market outcomes: a note that pays 7% if Nvidia is still the world's largest company in a year, backed by Kalshi hedges. Matt Levine walks through why this is inevitable: structured notes have always been 'bond plus embedded derivative,' and now that derivative can be a sports bet, a political bet, or a Fed rate cut bet.

The kicker: these products look like securities (CUSIP, custody account, favorable tax treatment) but feel like gambling. Your accountant sees 'structured note with embedded optionality' while you've effectively bet on the Rams winning the Super Bowl with principal protection.

"Your spouse or accountant... asks you about this note, you can be like 'oh yes, that's a structured note that I purchased from a reputable broker-dealer. It has a lot of embedded optionality. Uncorrelated returns! Convexity!'"

For Hiro: This is the financialization of everything — prediction markets becoming just another derivative underlying. For fintech, it's a fascinating distribution arbitrage: same payoff, different wrapper, different audience.
