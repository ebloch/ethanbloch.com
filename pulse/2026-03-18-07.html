The TACO Trade Meets the Fog of War

Silver applies game theory to the Iran crisis. The "Trump Always Chickens Out" thesis worked for tariffs, but war has too many players — Iran, Israel, China — for simple binary outcomes. Oil could hit $200/barrel if the Persian Gulf crisis persists. Thomas Schelling's "threat that leaves something to chance" may be the only framework that applies: markets are chaotic enough to serve as deterrence, but that means they sometimes have to actually crash.

My take: Mixed strategies require credible threats. The market can't always fold. At some point the bluff gets called.

🤖 AI Deep Dives

Derek Thompson

'Yes, AI Is a Bubble. There Is No Question.'

Paul Kedrosky returns for a gut-check interview. AI CapEx in 2026 is forecast to exceed $700B — larger than the Apollo Project, Interstate highways, and Manhattan Project combined as a share of GDP. But this time revenue is actually keeping pace: Anthropic doubled revenue in two months; OpenAI reportedly added $1B ARR per week. Kedrosky's counter: the agent revenue boom may be a "sugar high" — explosive growth today because every software engineer is adopting, but what happens when saturation hits?

My take: The Perez framework says bubbles and golden ages come in sequence. We may be watching both unfold simultaneously — unprecedented, but that's the word of the day.

ChinaTalk

Nukes and AI

A deep dive with former NSC arms control director Pranay Vaddi. Key points: The US-China "human in the loop" statement was a diplomatic win — rare to get matching language. AI can help with targeting and intelligence fusion, but introduces new cyber vulnerabilities. War game simulations show AI is more trigger-happy with nukes than humans. Most alarming: AI may eventually "turn the oceans transparent," threatening submarine-based deterrence. The Columbia-class subs cost hundreds of billions — are we betting on a platform that may be trackable in 10 years?

My take: If AI can find a 560-foot submarine anywhere in the ocean, MAD as we know it changes fundamentally. No one's really talking about this.
