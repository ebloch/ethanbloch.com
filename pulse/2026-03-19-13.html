Iran War Enters New Phase: Oil at $119, Rate Cut Hopes Evaporate

WSJ / Bloomberg

The Iran conflict escalated dramatically this week with Israeli strikes on the South Pars gas field and Iranian retaliation hitting Qatar's LNG infrastructure and launching missiles at Riyadh. Brent crude touched $119/barrel—economists peg $138 as the recession trigger.

The macro ripple effects are seismic. Fed Chair Powell made clear there will be no rate cuts until inflation cools, and bond traders now see even one 2026 cut as a coin flip. Europe is pricing in two ECB hikes. The S&P is down 3.7% since the war began, with deeper losses in Europe and Asia.

"China is light years ahead of the US in terms of energy, and energy is probably the key sector globally."

For Hiro: Macro backdrop matters for Hiro. If we're heading into a stagflationary environment—high energy costs, sticky inflation, no rate relief—the advice Hiro gives needs to reflect that reality. Also worth noting: $73B Samsung chip spend and Tesla/LG's $4.3B battery plant suggest the energy transition continues regardless.

MiniMax 2.7: GLM-5 Intelligence at One-Third the Cost

AINews

Two months post-IPO, MiniMax dropped M2.7—matching GLM-5's 50 on Artificial Analysis's Intelligence Index while costing just $0.30/$1.20 per 1M input/output tokens (vs GLM-5's ~$1/$4). That's the cost-performance frontier.

But the real story is the training approach: MiniMax calls it their "first model deeply participating in its own evolution," handling 30-50% of its own development workflow. Shades of Karpathy's Autoresearch vision. They're also pushing hard on agentic capabilities: 56.22% on SWE-Pro, 97% skill adherence across 40+ skills, and claimed parity with Sonnet 4.6 in OpenClaw benchmarks.

"M2.7 is our first model deeply participating in its own evolution."

For Hiro: The intelligence-per-dollar curve keeps compressing. For Hiro, this means the cost of running sophisticated financial analysis keeps dropping. My take: the "self-evolving" angle is early but watch this space—if models can meaningfully participate in their own improvement, we're in a new regime.
