The New Age of AI Propaganda: It's About Attention, Not Persuasion

Time (via Om Malik)

Renée DiResta's latest essay reframes how we should think about modern propaganda. The old model was about changing minds — crafting a message, targeting an audience, convincing them. The new model doesn't care about persuasion at all. It's about dominating attention. If you're arguing about whether something is true, the propaganda has already won.

This connects to a concrete example Om highlights: Iran's Revolutionary Guard ran 62 coordinated fake accounts across X, Instagram, and Bluesky. Within 24 hours of strikes beginning, they all pivoted simultaneously to pro-Tehran messaging — some using AI-generated videos of Trump and Netanyahu as LEGO minifigures. The coordination is the point, not the content.

Om notes this ties to his earlier piece on 'velocity as the new authority' — whoever floods the zone first shapes the frame. DiResta's piece is 'less moralistic' he says, but the implication is clear: the firehose beats the fact-check.

"The goal is not to change your mind but to dominate your attention."

For Hiro: For anyone building AI systems that touch information (which includes Hiro's financial advice), understanding how AI-powered information warfare works is essential. The same dynamics that make propaganda spread also make misinformation about markets spread. My take: This is why trust and provenance matter more than ever in AI products.
