Private Equity Is All-In on AI Transformation — And That Changes Everything

The Information

Jeff Bezos is raising $100 billion to buy manufacturing companies and automate them with AI. Meanwhile, Anthropic and OpenAI are both building joint ventures with PE giants (Blackstone, Hellman & Friedman, TPG, Brookfield, Bain Capital). This isn't just investment — it's distribution.

The top 10 PE firms own 2,000+ companies generating $2 trillion in revenue. Many are stuffed with software companies that look vulnerable to AI disruption. Rather than write them off, PE is going to force-feed them AI.

JLL expects $3 trillion in AI infrastructure spending through 2030. As Ken Brown notes: 'That doesn't sound as bonkers as it did a few months ago.'

"AI models, especially Anthropic's newest versions of Claude, coupled with OpenClaw—software for creating AI agents—have made AI's capabilities clear. What's also become clear is AI agents' insane demand for computing power."

For Hiro: This is the bridge from 'AI is impressive' to 'AI is everywhere.' PE firms have the capital, the portfolio companies, and the compensation incentives to push AI adoption aggressively. The companies that make AI adoption easy win.

When Your Borrower's AI Agent Makes the Default Decision

Fintech Takes

Alex Johnson's podcast with LendingClub's Chief Credit Officer and Plaid's Credit Lead surfaced a genuinely novel risk: what happens when consumers use AI agents to manage their finances, including strategic defaults?

A human might keep paying a lender they like or have history with. An AI agent optimizing purely for the borrower's financial outcome might make very different prioritization decisions. This isn't sci-fi — it's where agentic AI is heading.

The broader insight: credit underwriting should be viewed as an operating system, not a collection of models. LendingClub processes 90% of loans without human intervention. The remaining 10% is where AI can turn days into minutes.

"An AI agent, working on the human's behalf, may take a very different approach to that prioritization decision. This isn't science fiction. It's a logical extension of where agentic AI is heading."

For Hiro: Direct Hiro implications. If we're building AI that helps people with their finances, we need to think through how that changes the dynamics with lenders, investors, and the whole financial system. Also: credit-as-operating-system is a powerful framing.
