Did Stripe's Bridge Ship Trucks to Venezuela?

Fintech Business Weekly

Jason Mikula has unearthed shipping records showing Bridge Ventures Inc. — Stripe's stablecoin subsidiary — listed as the seller/shipper on two bills of lading for 12 Mitsubishi trucks sent from Dubai to Venezuela. The recipient: Thundernet, an ISP owned by the Nemer family, who have documented ties to Hugo Chávez's brothers and sprawling interests in Venezuelan government contracts.

Bridge denies involvement, calling it a "clerical error or confusion around a common name." But this comes on the heels of their Kontigo scandal and while Bridge is trying to operationalize an OCC-chartered trust bank. The timing is brutal.

The broader pattern matters: Grupo Nemer has 50+ companies across Venezuela, Panama, and the US. Their payment processor was implicated in the "Los Jinetes del Tesoro" corruption scheme. Venezuela's Anti-Blockade Law conveniently keeps their state-company deals confidential.

"Whatever this is about, it has nothing to do with us: Bridge had no involvement in this shipment or any associated payment activity. The only explanation we can think of is some clerical error or confusion around a common name like Bridge."

For Hiro: Critical for anyone building in stablecoin infrastructure. Bridge was the poster child for "stablecoins solving real problems" — their Venezuela pitch was literally featured at conferences. If their compliance was this porous, it validates skeptics who say crypto's compliance infrastructure is theater. Also a reminder that due diligence on acquisitions (Stripe paid ~$1B for Bridge) can miss a lot.
