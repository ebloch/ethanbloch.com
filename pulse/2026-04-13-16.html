Lead Stories

Anthropic's Mythos: The AI That Can Hack Everything

The Atlantic (via Crazy Stupid Tech)

The most significant AI story this week flew somewhat under the radar: Anthropic has built an AI model capable of finding and exploiting vulnerabilities in every major operating system and web browser. The capability is so alarming that Treasury Secretary Bessent and Fed Chair Powell summoned Wall Street leaders to an urgent meeting.

Anthropic's approach has been remarkably responsible — rather than releasing the model broadly, they've launched 'Project Glasswing' with Amazon, Apple, and JPMorgan to secure critical systems before similar capabilities become widely available. The company is essentially racing to patch the vulnerabilities their own AI discovered before others can replicate the capability.

"For the past several weeks, Anthropic says it secretly possessed a tool potentially capable of commandeering most computer servers in the world."

Microsoft's AI Infrastructure Stumble: How CFO Caution Let Rivals Leap Ahead

The Information

Ann Davis Vaughan's deep dive into Microsoft's AI infrastructure strategy reveals a cautionary tale of timing. In late 2024 and early 2025, as infrastructure capex looked set to exceed $80 billion, CFO Amy Hood pumped the brakes. The result: Microsoft walked away from or slowed down data center deals across the US and Europe, and competitors swooped in.

Google and Oracle grabbed the sites and utility queue positions Microsoft relinquished. Oracle secured big chunks of the Wisconsin capacity that was supposed to power OpenAI's flagship complex. Now Microsoft is scrambling with off-grid gas-fired solutions — deals with Crusoe, Chevron, and Nscale — which is somewhat ironic given their longstanding clean energy commitments.

"Microsoft is going to fall behind Google. Google has an amazing team. They continue to push."

  • Prediction Market Makers Aren't Neutral — They're Betting

    Bloomberg (Matt Levine)
