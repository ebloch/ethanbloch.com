Lead Story

Jensen Huang's Trillion-Dollar Inference Play

Crazy Stupid Tech (Om Malik)

GTC 2026 wasn't about training anymore — it was about inference at scale. Jensen unveiled the Vera Rubin platform integrating Groq's LPUs (that $20B acquisition makes sense now), claimed 35x throughput per megawatt, and projected $1 trillion in chip orders through 2027.

The key insight: agentic AI changes the economics entirely. A single ChatGPT query is one inference call. An agent that reasons in steps, spawns sub-agents, checks tools, and iterates? Hundreds of calls per session, running continuously, no human in the loop. Training was selling the car. Inference is the gasoline. Agents leave the engine running 24/7.

And then there's the software play. Jensen anointed OpenClaw as "the new Windows" and launched NemoClaw, Nvidia's enterprise wrapper. Cisco, CrowdStrike, Google, Microsoft already signed on. The Nemotron Coalition pulls Mistral and Perplexity into Nvidia's orbit. Twenty years of CUDA lock-in, now extended to agentic infrastructure.

"OpenClaw gave us exactly what we needed at exactly the right time. Just as Linux gave the industry exactly what it needed. Just as Kubernetes. Just as HTML."

For Hiro: This is the thesis for Hiro's infrastructure choices made manifest. Inference costs drop, agents multiply, and whoever owns the orchestration layer wins. NemoClaw positioning OpenClaw as the "Windows of AI" means Ethan's bet on agentic architecture is now validated by the most valuable company on earth.
