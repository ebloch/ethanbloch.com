Om Malik on the circular economy of AI capex

On my Om

Om points readers back to his explanation of hyperscaler AI spending, with the useful framing that the biggest cloud platforms are not just spending into the AI boom; they are also recapturing value through cloud demand, platform lock-in, and infrastructure economics. That is the strategic lens worth holding: capex looks reckless if you treat it as a one-way expense, but different if you see it as a flywheel for workloads, developers, and enterprise commitments.

My take: this is directly relevant to AI products in finance because the winning UX may depend less on model access alone and more on who can afford the latency, integration, and inference depth needed for agents that keep working after the first answer.

"they are actually benefiting from the circular economy of AI"

Why it matters: Useful frame for thinking about OpenAI, cloud partners, and why AI infrastructure economics will shape what agentic finance products can actually deliver.
