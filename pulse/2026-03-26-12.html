Arm's Historic Pivot: The IP Licensing Giant Is Now Selling Chips

Stratechery

In a move that fundamentally transforms the chip industry's ecosystem, Arm CEO Rene Haas announced at the company's first standalone keynote that the decades-long IP-licensing company will now sell its own chips. Meta is the first customer.

This is seismic. Arm's entire business model was built on being Switzerland — licensing to everyone without competing against anyone. Now they're fabless silicon designers like Nvidia or Qualcomm. The interview dives into why CPUs matter more than ever for AI (agents use tools designed for humans, and those tools run on CPUs), and how Arm's architecture compares to Nvidia's, x86, and custom silicon.

"You used to be someone special, I think you were the only CEO I talked to who did nothing other than license IP, now you're just another fabless chip guy like Jensen or Cristiano."

Novo Nordisk: AI Agents Are Shortening Clinical Trials by Months or Years

The Information

Novo Nordisk's Chief Digital Officer Stephanie Bova reveals that the Ozempic maker is using AI agents to coordinate clinical trials — and expecting to shave months or possibly years off drug approval timelines. They use Celonis software with Claude and OpenAI models to detect risks that could delay trials, auto-send Teams messages to leaders, and choose optimal locations for studies.

The financial math is staggering: 'A week of time saved in getting to market can mean tens of millions if not hundreds of millions of dollars in peak revenue impact.' They've already identified redundancies — three departments overseeing the same regulatory document drafting — and are replacing hundreds of contractors with data analysis agents.

But Bova is refreshingly honest about what doesn't work: they scaled back a Claude-powered tool for mining historical trial data because it was 'expensive to run and didn't seem to lead to many noticeable advances.'

"A week of time saved in getting to market can mean tens of millions if not hundreds of millions of dollars in peak revenue impact. So as long as we're shortening that timeline, I wouldn't even bother doing a business case calculation because the juice is clearly worth the squeeze."
